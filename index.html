An Introduction to
KLE Templates

Welcome! This is a guide to KLE templates. It'll explain what they are, why they're useful and how you can add one to your own KLE course. Don't panic, it's easier than you think!


If you're only looking to download a template, please click here.

What is a KLE template?

A template is a recommended layout, helping you to begin building your KLE course. By adding a template, you will populate the navigation menu with a pre-defined folder structure that's easy for students to navigate.


Most Keele tutors will need to apply a template before they begin uploading learning materials to the KLE.

Why were KLE templates introduced?

KLE templates were introduced in response to Keele University's I.T. survey, which highlighted some students having difficulty locating electronic resources. Templates are commonly used at other institutions and widely accepted as good practice.


They help to provide a more consistent experience across modules and make it easier for staff to deliver content in a style they feel comfortable with.

How do I know if I need a KLE template?

Most Keele tutors will need to apply a template before they begin uploading materials to the KLE, however there are exceptions.


Please find below a list of frequently asked questions to help you decide whether you require a KLE template. If you're still not sure, please speak to your local learning technology support person.


My School or subject area already use their own template.

In some schools or subject areas, adding a template before teaching begins is already standard practice and is managed in different ways. If this applies to your module, it is recommended that you speak to the person responsible for adding the template or get in touch with your local learning technology support contact to find out more.

I have already built a well structured KLE course in a previous year/semester.

It's important to remember that these templates are designed as a better starting point than what was previously available and do not intend to restrict innovation or replace existing good practice. If you have already spent a significant amount of time building a structure that works for you and your students then you may wish to use Blackboard's Course Copy feature instead.

I have a large amount of content to copy from a module I have taught previously.

Although it may be tempting to simply 'roll over' an entire module's content from one year to the next, it is often less time consuming to apply a fresh template and selectively copy individual folders or items rather than the whole course. By doing so, it avoids outdated content (e.g. module handbooks, reading lists, assignment details etc.) appearing in your new KLE module.

I used a template the last time I taught this module, why do I need to add one again?

A new blank KLE occurance is created each time a module is taught. Although the previous instance will still be available, students studying the module for the first time will be automatically added to the latest version.
